# 12 - Bottom Line

## In one paragraph

Anvil is a closed-loop orchestrator that narrows the class of agent-coding plugins down to its load-bearing discipline: machine-evaluable contracts, four-level evidence-gated verification, adversarial review in isolated context, and an append-only lesson store that makes every reset a durable improvement to the next contract. It replaces six predecessor systems not by covering more surface area but by refusing surface area - one loop, seven skills, five hooks, five commands, two human touchpoints. The bet is that a small system that insists on closing the loop outperforms a large system that documents it. The Ledger is the piece the predecessors did not have and the reason the bet converges instead of plateauing. The observability layer is the piece the predecessors forgot to build and the reason the bet is falsifiable. The Court is the piece the predecessors prototyped but never isolated properly, and the reason sycophancy has nowhere to hide.

## The one thing to remember

**Bad output is diagnostic, never product.** Patch-in-place is forbidden. Reset, write the lesson, re-run with a stricter contract. Repeat until the contract is strict enough that the agent has no ambiguity left to silently pick from. That is the whole plugin.

## The one thing to avoid

**Adding.** Every pressure to add a skill, a mode, a light-path, an advisory hook, a persona, a quick-path, or an optional gate must be resisted. New failure modes become rows in the failure taxonomy, lines in the Contract schema, or entries in the Ledger. The surface stays seven skills, five hooks, five commands, one CLI, one loop.

## Next step

Build stage 0 (bootstrap CLI + schemas + meta-skill). Feed stage 0 a seeded-fault test task. Watch the loop close. Build the rest under Anvil's own discipline.
